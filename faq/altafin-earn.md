# Alta Finance Earn

## **Why Earn?**

Earn makes it easy to have exposure to the underlying real estate asset revenue streams wrapped in a smart contract. Earn is available on Ethereum, Polygon and Avalanche with new chains launching regularly.

## **How does Alta Finance Earn work?**

Earn runs natively on Ethereum, Polygon, and Avalanche (BNB Chain, and other chains launching soon). All Earn contracts are opened with an accepted crypto.

* Open a yield-generating Earn contract using the following: USDC, USDT, DAI, FRAX, ALTA;
* Earn interest in the currency you lend according to the terms you select;
* Interest is redeemable daily during term lengths, and principal is unlocked at contract end;
* Term lengths are from thirty-six (36) to sixty (60) months;
* If you stake ALTA in your Earn contract, you can earn an interest rate multiplier bonus (x 1.15 or x 1.30);

## **How will my principal be returned?**

The principal will be returned in the same asset that was lent.

## **What if I need to close my position early?**

We have created a market to re-sell Earn contracts to 3rd parties, allowing any owner to exit their position at any time. Just place your contract For Sale and accept the highest bid on your terms. Bids and transfers will occur in the crypto used to open the contract.

## **How to change the current Network being used?**&#x20;

To switch between networks, go to your wallet and select the network that you want to use. Once you make that change in your wallet, the website will get updated and you will see the name of the network by your wallet address in the website's header.

## **What Networks do you support?**&#x20;

We currently support the Ethereum, Polygon, Avalanche mainnets. We also support Ropsten testnet.

## **How do I switch wallets?**&#x20;

To switch wallets, disconnect your current wallet from the connection. That will reload the website and show the "Connect to Wallet" button.

## **Is Alta Finance Earn open to everyone or only accredited investors (US)?**

Earn is open for anyone globally not in our Prohibited Jurisdictions. US citizens, due to the current regulatory environment, are within our Prohibited Jurisdictions. We are anxiously awaiting an update to the US regulatory climate.
